# Sample Clinical Guideline: BRCA1/2 Testing

## Indications for Genetic Testing

Genetic testing for BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations is recommended for:

- Personal history of breast cancer diagnosed at age ≤45
- Personal history of ovarian cancer
- Multiple primary breast cancers
- Male breast cancer
- Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry with breast or ovarian cancer
- Family history of known BRCA1/2 mutation
- Family history of multiple breast/ovarian cancers

## Management of Carriers

### High-Risk Surveillance

- Annual breast MRI and mammography from age 25–30
- Consider risk-reducing mastectomy (RRM) in selected cases
- Risk-reducing salpingo-oophorectomy (RRSO) recommended at age 35–40 (BRCA1) or 40–45 (BRCA2)

### PARP Inhibitors

- Olaparib and talazoparib approved for germline BRCA-mutated HER2-negative advanced breast cancer
- Olaparib maintenance after platinum response in ovarian cancer
